Critical Insights — What Matters Most:

1. Coverage:
These Top 15 setups capture approximately 90% of ALL measured move occurrences in the dataset. The remaining 10% are scattered across hundreds of lower-probability combinations. This means you can focus on just 15 patterns and catch nearly every high-probability setup.

2. Factor Hierarchy (Strongest → Weakest):
• Gap Size + Direction (largest impact) — Large gaps (>20% ABR) create powerful directional momentum
• IBS (strong predictor) — High/Low IBS shows conviction, mid IBS offers no edge
• Bar Size (inverse relationship) — Small bars predict expansion, large bars predict consolidation
• Bar Type (compression matters) — Inside bars coil energy, outside bars exhaust
• Prior Direction (moderate) — Continuation bias exists but is weaker than other factors

3. The Compression → Expansion Pattern:
Small bars and inside bars both represent compression and predict expansion. This pattern appears in nearly EVERY top setup (13 of 15). Quiet consolidation = coiled spring.

4. Triple Confirmation = Edge:
The 70%+ setups all combine THREE confirming signals: Compression (small/inside bar) + Conviction (strong IBS) + Confirmation (large gap). Any two factors provide an edge; all three create exceptional probability.

5. Bears Have More Edge:
The #1 setup (79.5%) is bearish. Bear setups dominate the top 15 (8 bear vs 7 bull). This confirms Section 1's finding that bears show more explosive potential — particularly with the Small Bar + Low IBS + Gap Down combination achieving 64.8% probability for a 1x measured move.

6. Sample Sizes Are Robust:
Top setups have n=120 to n=606. These are not curve-fit anomalies — they represent 3-14% of all trading days. The #1 bear setup occurs ~29 times per year. The #3 bull setup occurs ~144 times per year. These are highly tradable frequencies.
Research Synthesis & Key Findings
This comprehensive study analyzed 17 years of E-mini S&P 500 daily data to determine which prior bar characteristics predict measured move achievement. Through systematic analysis of direction, IBS, gap behavior, bar size, and bar type, we isolated high-probability setups with 70-80% edge.
Research Objective
Can we predict when measured moves (0.5x, 1x, 2x of yesterday's range) will be achieved by analyzing yesterday's bar characteristics? If so, which factors matter most and which combinations produce the highest probability?
Section 1 · Data Universe
Baseline Frequencies
Bull 0.5x: 26.9%, Bear 0.5x: 23.2%. Bears show 1.86× higher probability for 2x moves (3.1% vs 1.7%), indicating more explosive downside potential.
Section 2 · Prior Direction
Continuation Bias ~2:1
Prior bull bars: 34.6% bull 0.5x vs 16.1% bear. Prior bear bars: 31.5% bear 0.5x vs 17.1% bull. Continuation probability roughly doubles reversal probability.
Section 3 · Prior IBS
Strongest Single Factor
High IBS (>0.69): 37.0% bull vs 13.5% bear (2.74× ratio). Low IBS (<0.31): 35.4% bear vs 12.1% bull (2.93× ratio). Mid IBS offers no edge.
Section 4 · Gap Open
Gap Size Amplifies Edge
Gap Up + Large (>20% ABR): 50.3% bull vs 7.7% bear (6.53× ratio!). Gap Down + Large: 50.0% bear vs 6.5% bull (7.69× ratio!). Small gaps show no edge.
Section 5 · Prior Bar Size
Inverse Relationship
Small bars (<0.8x ABR): 38.4% bull / 30.0% bear. Large bars (1.2-2x): 12.9% bull / 14.0% bear. Compression → expansion pattern confirmed.
Section 6 · Bar Type
Inside Bars Dominate
Inside bars: 40.8% bull / 33.2% bear (highest of all types). Outside bars: 13.9% bull / 19.1% bear (lowest). Structure matters more than gap status.
🎯 The Universal Pattern: Compression → Expansion The single most important discovery: Small bars and inside bars both represent compression — tight ranges, low volatility, indecision. This compression creates coiled energy that releases into directional expansion. Conversely, large bars and outside bars represent exhaustion — the market has already moved significantly and needs to digest. This pattern appeared consistently across ALL analyses.

Trading implication: After quiet, narrow-range days, expect explosive moves. After big volatile days, expect consolidation. The market alternates between compression and expansion — anticipate which phase is next.
