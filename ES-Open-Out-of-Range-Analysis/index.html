Directional Symmetry: Both directions show similar overall gap closure rates (~65-67%), but opens below the low are slightly more likely to close the gap. The dominant pattern in both cases is the breakout bar, where price extends beyond one extreme while touching the other.
Step 3: The Critical Insight — Bar Type Mix by Gap Size

Executive Summary: The Compositional Shift
The transformation from small to large gaps represents one of the clearest patterns in price action analysis.

The Critical Threshold: The tipping point occurs between 25-50% of ABR. Below this range, breakout patterns dominate and gaps usually close. Above this range, No Overlap patterns dominate and gaps rarely close. For opens above high, the shift is dramatic: an 87 percentage point increase in Bull No Overlap from smallest to largest gaps. For opens below low: a 73 percentage point increase in Bear No Overlap.
Trading Implication: Bar Type acts as a real-time confirmation signal. When you see a large gap (>50% ABR) form a Bull No Overlap or Bear No Overlap bar, the probability the gap stays open through the session exceeds 70%. Conversely, small gaps (<25% ABR) that form Bull Breakout or Bear Breakout bars have an 80%+ chance of closing.
