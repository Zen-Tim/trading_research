Methodology: A consecutive opening bar pattern occurs when the first two 60-minute bars of the trading session (08:00 and 09:00 CET) are both bullish (close > open) or both bearish (close < open). We measure the directional correlation between this 2-hour pattern and the final daily bar outcome (daily close vs daily open).

Step 5: Headroom Analysis — How Much Room is Left After the Pattern?

Directional correlation is only useful if there's practical "headroom" remaining to capitalize on. We measure headroom as the distance from the close of Bar 2 (09:00 CET, when the pattern completes) to the daily extreme in the predicted direction. For bull patterns, headroom = Daily High - Close of Bar 2. For bear patterns, headroom = Close of Bar 2 - Daily Low.

Headroom Definition: The first two 60-minute bars consume an average of 63.7% of the daily average bar range by 09:00 CET. This leaves 43.8% of the daily ABR as "headroom" — the remaining distance to the daily extreme in the predicted direction. The headroom multiple of 0.78× means that on average, the remaining upside/downside is about three-quarters the size of the initial 2-bar pattern. Critically, in zero cases did the pattern exceed the daily extreme by 09:00, confirming there is always some headroom remaining.

Executive Summary: Key Findings

Primary Finding: 79.9% Predictive Accuracy

When the first two 60-minute bars of the FDAX trading session are both bullish or both bearish, the daily bar closes in that same direction 79.9% of the time. This represents a statistically significant directional correlation across 962 pattern occurrences over 1,960 trading days (49.1% pattern frequency).

Perfect Bull/Bear Symmetry

FDAX demonstrates nearly perfect structural balance with a 1.03× bull/bear ratio (488 vs 474 patterns), far more symmetric than ES futures (1.23× bull bias). Bulls show modest +2.5% accuracy advantage (81.1% vs 78.7%), making them both slightly more frequent AND slightly more reliable, but the difference is minimal compared to ES.

State-Specific Performance

State +1 (higher high, higher low, close above prior high) and State -1 (lower high, lower low, close below prior low) are the dominant patterns, representing 57% of all consecutive days. State -1 shows the highest accuracy at 85.0% (266 cases), while State +1 achieves 84.8% accuracy (282 cases). Just 6 states (±1, ±2, ±5) account for 96.6% of all patterns, demonstrating high concentration in clean directional setups.

Headroom Constraints

The first two hours consume an average of 63.7% of the daily ABR by 09:00 CET, leaving 43.8% as headroom (0.78× pattern size). Bear patterns show superior headroom multiples (0.83×) despite consuming more range, while bulls average 0.73×. The median headroom of 0.54× indicates right-skewed distribution with occasional large continuation days. This validates a critical execution consideration: the first two hours capture significant range, requiring careful target setting.

Bar 2 Entry Quality

Entering at Bar 2's close (09:00 CET) with stops at Bar 2's range provides 1.45× average reward:risk (median 1.05×) — a significant improvement over pattern-based metrics. Bears offer superior 1.53× multiples versus bulls at 1.38×. Both directions show positive median multiples, meaning more than half of cases offer better than 1:1 reward:risk. This creates practical implementability for trend continuation trades using Bar 2 range as the stop reference.

FDAX vs ES Comparison

FDAX shows slightly lower overall accuracy than ES (79.9% vs 82.2%) but demonstrates superior structural symmetry (1.03× vs 1.23× bull bias). Bear patterns underperform more on FDAX (78.7% vs ES's 81.2%), while bulls show comparable performance (81.1% vs 82.6%). The Bar 2 headroom multiple is notably better on FDAX (1.45× vs ES's 1.20×), suggesting FDAX offers superior entry quality despite marginally lower directional accuracy.

Recommended Next Steps

This study establishes directional correlation, identifies the highest-probability states, quantifies headroom constraints, and validates Bar 2 entry quality. The next phase should investigate: (1) Entry timing optimization — comparing immediate entry at 09:00 versus waiting for pullbacks to Bar 2's midpoint or low to improve risk-reward, (2) Context dependency — whether accuracy or headroom varies by gap size, prior day type, or volatility regime, (3) Time-to-target analysis — when during the day these patterns typically resolve to daily extremes, and (4) MFE/MAE validation — realistic profit targets and maximum adverse excursion given the 1.45× Bar 2 headroom baseline.
