Avenue 1 verdict — hypothesis disproved: Every hit rate is lower on ETH data, not higher. The 1x rate drops from 11.1% to 6.1% (bull) and 11.6% to 7.7% (bear). Bear 2x ratio: 2.69x

Finding 1: Compression → Expansion is universal

The pattern holds across ETH and RTH sessions. Small bars and inside bars predict expansion; large bars and outside bars predict consolidation. This is the foundational principle.

Finding 2: ETH amplifies factor separation

Every factor shows stronger differentiation on ETH data. Gap size advantage jumps from 6–8x (RTH) to 34–48x (ETH). Bar size differential widens from 3x to 5x. The signal is cleaner.

Finding 3: Factor hierarchy shifts slightly

Bar size (compression) rises from #3 to #2, swapping with IBS. On the 24-hour session, whether yesterday's range was small relative to average matters more than where it closed.

Finding 4: Bear asymmetry intensifies

The 2x bear/bull ratio widens from 1.86x (RTH) to 2.69x (ETH). Bear setups dominate the Top 15 (9 bear vs 6 bull). Overnight sessions amplify downside tail risk.

Finding 5: The 1x edge exists in concentrated setups

While baseline 1x rates are dismal (6–8%), the Small Bar + Large Gap combination produces 44–46% 1x rates. This makes the 1x target tradable at ~1:1 R:R with a genuine 45% win rate — thin but structurally sound.

Finding 6: Frequency is the trade-off

The highest-probability setups occur only 1.3–2.5 times per year. Broader setups (Small Bar + Low IBS) offer 16.9 opportunities per year at 47.9% 0.5x / 25.3% 1x — a better frequency/probability trade-off for active traders.

Recommendations: (1) The ETH session does NOT improve baseline measured move rates — Avenue 1 hypothesis is disproved. (2) However, ETH data reveals cleaner factor separation, especially for gap + compression combinations. (3) The Small Bar + Large Gap setup on ETH achieves ~45% at the 1x level — worth investigating further with entry/exit rules. (4) Proceed to Avenue 3 (ABR from Close) as the primary path for improving baseline rates. (5) Consider developing a hybrid: use RTH for baseline signals but monitor ETH for the rare Small Bar + Large Gap setups that have dramatically higher 1x potential.
