Primary finding — hypothesis confirmed: ABR from prior close produces dramatically higher baseline hit rates. Bull 1x rises from 11.1% to 18.7% (+68%), Bear 1x from 11.6% to 20.3% (+75%). At ~1 per week each direction, these are structurally tradable frequencies. The 0.5x rates nearly doubled (47.7%/44.9% vs 26.9%/23.2%). The 2x rates are slightly lower — expected, as 2× ABR from the close is a larger absolute move on most days.
Why the rates are higher: Two structural changes drive the improvement. First, the anchor shifts from the prior extreme to the prior close, which is typically interior to the bar — this shortens the distance to target. Second, ABR smooths away outlier bars — a single large day no longer inflates tomorrow’s targets.

After Bear Bars: Everything Hits More

After a bear bar, both bull and bear rates are elevated (51.4%/47.9% vs 44.7%/42.3%). This is volatility clustering — bear bars precede more volatile next days, lifting hit rates in both directions.

IBS = Volatility Proxy, Not Directional

Low IBS (closed near bar low) lifts all hit rates — bull and bear alike. The highest Bull 1x rate (26.3%) is at IBS 10–20%. This was the “strongest single predictor” in the original study; under close-anchored targets it loses all directional power.

Monotonic Decline from Low to High IBS

Bull hit rates decline almost monotonically from low to high IBS: 56.4% → 44.0% (0.5x) and 26.3% → 15.3% (1x). Low IBS = more volatile next day = more targets hit in both directions.

Why the gap dominates: Under ABR-from-close, targets are measured from yesterday’s close. The gap IS a head start toward the target. A gap up of 30% ABR means the market opens already 30% of the way to the 1x target. At >30% ABR, the gap distribution has a long right tail — many days open past the 0.5x target, hence the 91–93% hit rates.
Bear asymmetry intensifies: Bear 1x at 55.8% vs Bull 1x at 48.7% for >30% gaps. Bear 1.5x at 27.2% vs Bull 1.5x at 15.8%. Downside moves are more explosive — this is a real market property, not an artifact.

Pattern inverted — expansion predicts expansion: The compression → expansion pattern is REVERSED. Small bars produce the lowest rates (44.2%/40.1%), while large and very large bars produce the highest (52.1–56.4% / 47.8–53.5%). ABR smooths volatility, so when a prior bar is large relative to ABR, it signals expanding volatility — and volatility clusters. The next day also tends to be volatile, lifting hit rates in both directions.

Small Bars: Below Baseline

Small prior bars (<0.8x ABR) produce rates below baseline in every metric. In the original study these were the “coiled energy” setups at 38%+. Under close-anchored targets, small bars mean low volatility, which depresses next-day range expectations.

Very Large Bars: Both Sides Elevated

After very large bars (>2x ABR), both bull (56.4%) and bear (53.5%) 0.5x rates are well above baseline. Bull 1x reaches 32.7%. This is pure volatility clustering — extreme days beget extreme days.

BL NOL is the outlier: Bull No Overlap bars (measuring gaps up) produce the lowest rates by far — 36.1% in both directions, 8.6% Bull 1x. Bear Breakout: Highest Rates

Bear breakout bars produce the highest rates: 52.2%/48.7% at 0.5x, 23.3%/24.7% at 1x. Like bear direction and low IBS, this is a volatility signal — a down breakout signals expanding volatility in both directions.

Inside Bar Edge Neutralised

Inside bars were the top bar type in the original study at 40%+. Here they are at baseline (47.7%/47.0%). The compression → expansion edge required extreme-anchored targets to manifest.

No non-gap setup exceeds baseline significantly: Without gap direction, no combination of IBS + bar size + bar type produces a 1x rate above ~26%. The gap is a necessary condition for any actionable 1x setup in the ABR-from-close framework.
Section 08Executive Summary

Synthesis of findings and comparison with the original range-from-extreme study.

Headline: ABR from prior close nearly doubles 1x hit rates (11% → 19–20%) and reveals that gap direction is the only factor that matters. The original study’s factor hierarchy (Gap > IBS > Bar Size > Bar Type > Direction) collapses to a single dominant factor: gap direction + size. All other factors lose their directional power and become volatility proxies under the close anchor.

1. Hypothesis Confirmed

Bull 1x: 11.1% → 18.7% (+68%). Bear 1x: 11.6% → 20.3% (+75%). The close anchor + ABR smoothing produces structurally better baseline rates at every level except 2x.

2. Gap Dominance

Gap direction alone produces 71–73% at 0.5x and 31–37% at 1x. Gap direction captures 92% of bull 1x hits and 80% of bear 1x hits. No other factor comes close.

3. All Other Factors Neutralised

Direction (~2:1 → ~1:1), IBS (2.7x edge → ~1x), compression → expansion (inverted), inside bar edge (gone). These were artifacts of extreme-anchored targets, not genuine market signals.

4. Bear Asymmetry Persists

Bear 2x/Bull 2x = 2.68x (vs 1.86x original). Bear 1x rates consistently exceed bull 1x rates at every gap size. Downside momentum is more explosive — a real market property, not an artifact.

5. Factor Hierarchy Simplifies

Original: Gap > IBS > Bar Size > Bar Type > Direction. New: Gap >>> (everything else ≈ volatility noise). Secondary factors add 5–10pp but carry no standalone directional power.

6. Tradable 1x Setups Exist

Gap Down >30% + Bear BO: 64.5% at 1x, 16/yr. Gap Up >30% + Low IBS: 53.9% at 1x, 19/yr. These are ~50%+ win rates at ~1:1 R:R with sufficient frequency.

What this means for trading: The ABR-from-close framework converts the measured move study into what is essentially a gap continuation study. The tradable insight is simple: when the market gaps >30% of ABR, expect price to travel at least 1× ABR from the prior close in the gap direction roughly half the time (49–56%), and at least 0.5× ABR over 90% of the time. The open itself does most of the work — the gap IS the measured move in many cases.
Next steps: (1) Investigate whether the gap continuation edge has a time component — does most of the 1x move happen in the first 30–60 minutes? (2) Test Avenue 2 (pullback entry) on gap days to improve R:R further. (3) Develop concrete entry/exit rules: entry at open on gap days >30% ABR, target at 1x ABR from close, stop TBD. (4) Backtest with realistic slippage assumptions.
