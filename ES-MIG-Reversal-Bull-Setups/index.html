Trade Logic: Bull MIG Reversals are entered at the close of the signal bar (when 3+ consecutive bull bars create a micro-gap and the signal bar's low is below 20 EMA). Stop is placed 1 tick below the lowest low of the entire consecutive sequence. Trade is tracked until: (1) Stop hit, (2) Profit target hit, or (3) End of day (closed 2nd last bar). Each setup is measured against multiple R-multiple targets (0.5R, 1R, 1.5R, 2R, 3R) to determine optimal profit-taking levels.

Step 2: Optimal Target Selection — Expectancy Analysis

To determine the optimal profit target, we calculate expectancy using actual R-multiples for all outcomes. Trades that hit target generate their full R-multiple (+0.5R, +1R, +1.5R, or +2R). Trades that hit stop generate -1R. Trades that reach end-of-day without hitting either target or stop are closed at the 2nd-last bar, and actual R-multiple is calculated based on final price relative to entry.

Filter Performance: More Than Doubles Expectancy at 2R

The "Close Below EMA20 + 3-Bar" filter produces substantial improvements across all target levels. At 2R, expectancy more than doubles from +0.101R to +0.208R (+0.107R improvement), while hit rate improves from 22.0% to 28.9%. The tradeoff: frequency reduces from 0.88 to 0.26 setups per day (70% reduction).

Practical Application: The filter reduces setup frequency from 894 to 263 (70% reduction) while improving edge quality. Traders using this filter would see approximately 0.26 setups per day (263 setups / 1,017 days) versus 0.88 per day for all bulls. The selectivity tradeoff is justified by the improved win rates and expectancy — particularly at the 1R and 1.5R targets, where the filter creates positive mathematical edge.

The "Disappointment" Thesis is Validated

The data strongly supports the core "disappointment" concept: bull micro-gaps that occur BELOW the 20 EMA (bears disappointed by bulls' strength in unfavorable territory) represent genuine reversals with strong follow-through. In contrast, bull micro-gaps occurring ABOVE the 20 EMA (bulls continuing in favorable territory) are essentially noise with minimal edge. The majority of bull setups (60.7%) close above the EMA, but these contribute almost nothing to overall profitability. The entire edge exists in the 39.3% minority that close below the EMA. This validates the filtering approach in Step 3 and suggests that "Close Below EMA20" should be considered a mandatory filter rather than an optional enhancement.

Executive Summary: Key Findings

Primary Finding: 2R Target Optimal, All Targets Positive

Across all 894 bull MIG reversals, the 2R profit target produces the highest expectancy at +0.101R per trade, with 22.0% target hit rate and 44.4% win rate (using actual R-multiples for EOD positions). All tested targets show positive expectancy: 0.5R (+0.026R), 1R (+0.062R), 1.5R (+0.077R), and 2R (+0.101R). The 0.5R target offers the highest hit rate (63.4%) for traders prioritizing win frequency, while the 2R target maximizes expectancy per trade for traders willing to accept lower hit rates in exchange for better reward:risk when trades do work.

Excursion Characteristics

Bull setups show average MFE of 1.25R (median 0.77R) and average MAE of -0.82R (median -0.93R). This indicates that while setups have directional edge, they typically experience nearly -1R drawdown before resolution. The average setup is tracked for 41.5 bars (207 minutes), with positions closed at the 2nd-last bar of the day if neither target nor stop is hit. EOD closure rates vary by target: 8.5% at 0.5R, 17.9% at 1R, 26.7% at 1.5R, and 33.1% at 2R — demonstrating that higher targets often require the full session to resolve, with actual R-multiple at close factored into expectancy.

Filter Performance: Close Below EMA20 + 3-Bar Patterns

Filtering for setups where the signal bar closes below 20 EMA combined with 3-bar patterns (29.4% of population, n=263) produces substantial improvements. At 2R: expectancy more than doubles from +0.101R to +0.208R (+0.107R improvement), with hit rate improving from 22.0% to 28.9% (+6.9%). At 1R: expectancy doubles from +0.062R to +0.129R (+0.067R improvement), with hit rate improving from 43.8% to 50.2% (+6.3%). Filter improvement scales with target size: +0.039R at 0.5R, +0.067R at 1R, +0.083R at 1.5R, and +0.107R at 2R. Average MFE increases to 1.49R (+0.24R improvement), suggesting these filtered setups have stronger directional follow-through. The filter reduces frequency from 0.88 to 0.26 setups per day but meaningfully improves edge quality.

Target Selection Strategy

For maximum expectancy (all bulls): Use 2R target for +0.101R expectancy and 44.4% win rate. Accept 22.0% target hit rate with 0.88 setups/day. The 0.5R target offers highest hit rate (63.4%) but lowest expectancy (+0.026R) for traders prioritizing win frequency over profit per trade.

For quality-filtered approach: Apply "Close Below EMA20 + 3-Bar" filter and use 2R target for +0.208R expectancy (best performance), or 1.5R for +0.160R expectancy (more frequent resolution). Accept 0.26 setups/day with superior edge — filter more than doubles expectancy at all target levels.

Key Finding: All targets from 0.5R to 2R show positive expectancy across both filtered and unfiltered populations. Filter improvement scales with target size, making 2R particularly attractive for filtered setups (+107% expectancy improvement vs +150% at 0.5R).

Disappointment Thesis Validated: Edge Exists in "Close Below" Only

Bull MIG patterns that close BELOW the 20 EMA dramatically outperform those closing ABOVE across all targets. At 2R: "Close Below" produces +0.209R expectancy versus only +0.031R for "Close Above" — a 6.7× performance advantage. At 1R: 9.7× better (+0.136R vs +0.014R). The "Close Above" setups (60.7% of all bulls) are essentially breakeven, while the entire edge exists in the 39.3% minority that close below the EMA. Average MFE for "Close Below" is 1.714R versus 1.301R for "Close Above" (+0.413R difference). This validates the core "disappointment" concept: bears disappointed by bulls' strength below trend represents a genuine reversal signal, while bulls continuing above trend is noise. "Close Below EMA20" should be considered a mandatory filter, not an optional enhancement.

Recommended Next Steps

This analysis establishes baseline performance for bull MIG reversals and validates the "Close Below EMA20 + 3-Bar" filter. Next phase should investigate: (1) Bear MIG Reversal performance using identical methodology to determine if bears show similar or superior characteristics, (2) Additional filter combinations such as EMA200 alignment, first-12-bars timing, or 4-bar patterns, (3) Entry timing optimization to reduce MAE exposure (potentially waiting for pullback entry versus immediate close entry), and (4) Scaling strategies where partial profits are taken at 0.5R with remainder targeting 1R or 1.5R to capture both high-probability small wins and occasional larger moves.
