Methodology: A consecutive opening bar pattern occurs when the first two 60-minute bars of the trading session (09:30 and 10:30 EST) are both bullish (close > open) or both bearish (close < open). We measure the directional correlation between this 2-hour pattern and the final daily bar outcome (daily close vs daily open).

Executive Summary: Key Findings

Primary Finding: 82.2% Predictive Accuracy

When the first two 60-minute bars of the ES trading session are both bullish or both bearish, the daily bar closes in that same direction 82.2% of the time. This represents a statistically significant directional correlation across 1,033 pattern occurrences over 2,045 trading days (50.5% pattern frequency).

Bull Pattern Correlation

Consecutive bull patterns (n=570) predict bullish daily outcomes with 82.6% accuracy. After identifying two consecutive green 60-minute bars by 10:30 EST, there is strong statistical evidence for daily continuation. Bulls occur 23% more frequently than bears and show 1.4% superior accuracy, making them the more prevalent pattern.

Bear Pattern Correlation

Consecutive bear patterns (n=463) predict bearish daily outcomes with 81.2% accuracy. While slightly less accurate than bulls, bears still provide a strong directional correlation. The 18.8% incorrect rate is comparable to the bull pattern's 17.0% incorrect rate, suggesting both patterns offer similar predictive value.

Pattern Frequency & Timing

Consecutive opening bars occur on 50.5% of all trading days, providing substantial sample size for statistical analysis. The pattern completes by 10:30 EST (second 60-minute bar close), allowing for same-day outcome measurement within a 5+ hour window. This creates a clear causal timeframe — pattern identified at 10:30, outcome measured at 16:00 session close.

State-Specific Performance

State +1 (higher high, higher low, close above prior high) and State -1 (lower high, lower low, close below prior low) are the dominant patterns, representing 64% of all consecutive days. State -1 shows the highest accuracy at 86.6% (268 cases), while State +1 achieves 85.4% accuracy (392 cases). Four "Excellent Tier" states (±1, +2, -5) maintain >82% accuracy while covering 75% of all patterns, demonstrating that the strongest correlations come from clean directional continuation with strong range expansion.

Headroom Constraints

The first two hours consume an average of 68.8% of the daily ABR by 10:30 EST, leaving 40.8% as headroom (0.66× pattern size). An inverse relationship exists: small patterns (<50% ABR) leave 0.88× headroom, while large patterns (>75% ABR) leave only 0.47× headroom. Bear patterns show superior headroom multiples (0.74×) despite consuming more range, while bulls average 0.59×. State -5 offers the best headroom at 0.91×, nearly equal to pattern size. This validates a critical execution filter: prioritize patterns consuming <50-75% of daily ABR to maximize practical runway.

Recommended Next Steps

This study establishes directional correlation, identifies the highest-probability states, and quantifies practical headroom constraints. The next phase should investigate: (1) Entry timing optimization — should positions be entered immediately at 10:30 or wait for pullbacks to improve risk-reward, (2) Context dependency — does accuracy or headroom vary based on gap size, prior day type, or volatility regime, (3) Time-to-target analysis — when during the day do these patterns typically resolve to the daily extreme, and (4) MFE/MAE validation — what are realistic profit targets and stop distances given the headroom constraints identified.

Step 5: Headroom Analysis — How Much Room is Left After the Pattern?

Directional correlation is only useful if there's practical "headroom" remaining to capitalize on. We measure headroom as the distance from the close of Bar 2 (10:30 EST, when the pattern completes) to the daily extreme in the predicted direction. For bull patterns, headroom = Daily High - Close of Bar 2. For bear patterns, headroom = Close of Bar 2 - Daily Low.

Headroom Definition: The first two 60-minute bars consume an average of 68.8% of the daily average bar range by 10:30 EST. This leaves 40.8% of the daily ABR as "headroom" — the remaining distance to the daily extreme in the predicted direction. The headroom multiple of 0.66× means that on average, the remaining upside/downside is about two-thirds the size of the initial 2-bar pattern. Critically, in zero cases did the pattern exceed the daily extreme by 10:30, confirming there is always some headroom remaining.

Practical Implications

The headroom analysis reveals a critical execution constraint: while 82.2% directional accuracy exists, the first two hours consume 68.8% of the daily range on average, leaving only 40.8% as headroom (0.66× pattern size). This ratio worsens for large opening patterns — when the first 2 hours exceed 75% of daily ABR (33.7% of cases), headroom drops to just 0.47×. Conversely, smaller patterns (0-50% of ABR, 25.2% of cases) leave 0.88× headroom, nearly equal to pattern size. This suggests a filtering criterion: prioritize patterns where the 2-bar range is <50% of daily ABR to maximize headroom-to-pattern ratios. State-level variations exist, with bear states generally providing superior headroom multiples despite larger pattern consumption.
