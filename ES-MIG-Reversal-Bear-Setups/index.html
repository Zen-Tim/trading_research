Trade Logic: Bear MIG Reversals are entered at the close of the signal bar (when 3+ consecutive bear bars create a micro-gap and the signal bar's high is above 20 EMA). Stop is placed 1 tick above the highest high of the entire consecutive sequence. Trade is tracked until: (1) Stop hit, (2) Profit target hit, or (3) End of day (closed 2nd last bar, actual R-multiple calculated). Each setup is measured against multiple R-multiple targets (0.5R, 1R, 1.5R, 2R) to determine optimal profit-taking levels.
Average Bars Tracked: This measures how many 5-minute bars elapse from entry until the trade resolves (target hit, stop hit, or EOD). At 41.9 bars average, most bear setups take approximately 3.5 hours (210 minutes) to reach their outcome. This is important for understanding holding time requirements and intraday capital commitment.

Critical Insight: The filter reduces setup frequency from 844 to 344 (59% reduction) while dramatically improving edge quality at 1R and beyond. Unlike the bull "disappointment" filter, bear edge comes from CONTINUATION below trend, not reversal above it. Traders should use this filter for bears targeting 1R+ and accept the 41% lower frequency in exchange for 2-3× better expectancy.

The "Disappointment" Hypothesis FAILS for Bears

The data contradicts the "disappointment" concept for bear setups. While bull patterns showed 6-10× better performance when closing BELOW EMA20 (bears disappointed), bear patterns show the opposite: closing BELOW EMA20 (bears continuing in favorable territory) produces superior results at 1R, 1.5R, and 2R targets. At 2R, the gap is massive: +0.127R (Below) vs -0.040R (Above). The "disappointment" setup for bears (Close Above) only works at 0.5R and shows negative expectancy beyond that. This asymmetry suggests different market dynamics: bear continuations (Close Below) are more reliable than bear reversals (Close Above), the opposite pattern from bulls.

Executive Summary: Key Findings

Primary Finding: 2R Target Optimal, But Much Weaker Than Bulls

Across all 844 bear MIG reversals, the 2R profit target produces the highest expectancy at +0.059R per trade, with 40.3% win rate and 59.7% loss rate. All tested targets show positive expectancy: 0.5R (+0.013R), 1R (+0.028R), 1.5R (+0.055R), and 2R (+0.059R). However, bear expectancy is consistently lower than bulls — at 2R, bears produce +0.059R versus bulls' +0.101R (only 58% of bull performance). Bears show stronger average MFE (1.68R vs 1.25R for bulls) but worse MAE (-1.44R vs -0.82R), suggesting more volatile price action with larger drawdowns before resolution.

Filter Performance: Close Below Doubles Expectancy at 2R

The "Close Below EMA20 + 3-Bar" filter (n=344, 40.8% of bears) produces dramatic improvements at longer targets. At 2R: expectancy more than doubles from +0.059R to +0.152R (+0.093R improvement), with win rate improving from 40.3% to 43.6%. At 1.5R: expectancy more than doubles from +0.055R to +0.138R (+0.083R improvement). At 1R: expectancy increases from +0.028R to +0.074R (+0.046R improvement). The filter only weakens 0.5R (-0.002R). This is the OPPOSITE pattern from bulls — for bears, "Close Below" is continuation with trend (not disappointment), which provides superior follow-through. The filter reduces frequency 59% but creates 2-3× better expectancy at meaningful targets.

Disappointment Hypothesis FAILS: Close Below Dominates at 2R

Unlike bulls, bear patterns show opposite behavior regarding EMA position. Close BELOW EMA20 (bears continuing in favorable territory) dramatically outperforms Close ABOVE at longer targets. At 2R: Close Below produces +0.127R versus -0.040R for Close Above (3.2× better). At 1.5R: +0.090R versus +0.003R (30× better). Only at 0.5R does Close Above show advantage (+0.033R vs -0.001R). This contradicts the "disappointment" thesis that worked for bulls. Bear continuations (below trend) are more reliable than bear reversals (above trend), suggesting fundamentally different market dynamics between bull and bear setups. The best bear filter is "Close Below + 3-Bar" which produces +0.152R at 2R.

Target Selection Strategy

For maximum expectancy: Use "Close Below EMA20 + 3-Bar" filter with 2R target for +0.152R expectancy (best performance) and 43.6% win rate. At 1.5R, this filter produces +0.138R with 47.4% win rate. Avoid "Close Above" bears at targets beyond 0.5R (negative expectancy at 1R and 2R). Unfiltered bears work but show only +0.059R at 2R.

Key Insight: Bears are NOT mirror images of bulls. The disappointment pattern that drives bull edge does not apply to bears. For bears, continuation below trend (Close Below EMA20) provides the strongest edge at meaningful targets, while reversal setups (Close Above) are quick scalps only. This is the OPPOSITE of bulls where Close Below represents reversal/disappointment.

Recommended Next Steps

This analysis reveals critical asymmetries between bull and bear MIG reversals. Next phase should investigate: (1) Why bear continuations outperform bear reversals while bulls show the opposite pattern, (2) Alternative bear filters focused on "Close Below + large MFE" rather than applying bull logic, (3) Combined bull/bear strategies using different filters for each direction (Bulls: Close Below filter, Bears: Close Below or unfiltered), and (4) Entry timing for bears to reduce the -1.44R average MAE exposure, particularly for Close Above setups that show extreme -1.76R MAE.
