We measure the directional correlation between this 2-hour pattern and the final daily bar outcome (daily close vs daily open).

Step 5: Headroom Analysis — How Much Room is Left After the Pattern?

Directional correlation is only useful if there's practical "headroom" remaining to capitalize on. We measure headroom as the distance from the close of Bar 2 (when the pattern completes) to the daily extreme in the predicted direction. For bull patterns, headroom = Daily High - Close of Bar 2. For bear patterns, headroom = Close of Bar 2 - Daily Low.

Executive Summary: Key Findings

Primary Finding: 88.8% Predictive Accuracy

When the first two 60-minute bars of the HSI trading session are both bullish or both bearish, the daily bar closes in that same direction 88.8% of the time. This represents exceptionally strong directional correlation across 937 pattern occurrences over 1,877 trading days (49.9% pattern frequency). HSI outperforms ES by 6.6 percentage points (88.8% vs 82.2%), making it one of the most reliable consecutive opening bar markets analyzed.

Bull Pattern Correlation

Consecutive bull patterns (n=450) predict bullish daily outcomes with 88.4% accuracy. Bulls occur slightly less frequently than bears (0.92× ratio), contrasting with ES's 1.23× bull bias. The 11.6% incorrect rate is comparable across both directions, indicating reliable predictive value despite the bear frequency advantage.

Bear Pattern Correlation

Consecutive bear patterns (n=487) predict bearish daily outcomes with 89.1% accuracy — marginally superior to bulls (+0.7%). Bears show a dual advantage: they occur 8% more frequently AND predict daily direction slightly more reliably. The 10.9% incorrect rate represents the lowest failure rate observed, highlighting HSI's strong bear-side predictability.

State-Specific Performance

State +1 (HH HL, CL>H[1]) and State -1 (LH LL, CL92.9% accuracy (282 cases) while State -1 achieves 92.2% accuracy (307 cases) — both significantly exceeding ES's ±1 state performance. ALL six high-frequency states (±1, ±2, ±5) maintain "Excellent Tier" status with 80%+ accuracy, covering 97% of patterns. This represents remarkably uniform excellence — no "moderate tier" states exist, unlike ES where accuracy ranges from 72-87%.

Headroom Constraints

The first two hours consume an average of 81.5% of the daily ABR by Bar 2 completion, leaving only 34.2% as headroom (0.46× pattern size). This represents significantly tighter constraints than ES (68.8% consumed, 0.66× multiple). An inverse relationship exists: small patterns (<50% ABR, only 7.9% of cases) leave 0.72× headroom, while large patterns (>75% ABR, 54.2% of cases) leave only 0.34× headroom. Bulls and bears show remarkable symmetry, both consuming ~81.5% of ABR with identical 0.36× median multiples. The dominant States ±1 offer only 0.40× headroom despite exceptional directional accuracy, while inside bar states (±5) provide superior 0.57-0.61× multiples.

Bar 2 Entry Quality

Entering at Bar 2's close with stops at Bar 2's range provides 0.90× average reward:risk (median 0.69×) — falling short of 1:1 and notably worse than ES's 1.20× multiple. Bulls and bears show symmetric entry quality at 0.91× and 0.89× respectively. Bull bars with LOW IBS (0-30%) provide the best headroom at 1.30×, while high IBS bulls (70-100%, the majority at 60.4%) offer only 0.83×. Bears show optimal headroom at weak closes (70-100% IBS: 1.27×) though these are rare (3.3% of cases). State +5 provides exceptional 1.27× headroom (0.93× median), while State -5 offers 1.16×. The dominant States ±1 provide only 0.78-0.79× despite their 92%+ directional accuracy, creating a critical frequency vs quality tradeoff.

HSI vs ES Comparison

Directional Superiority: HSI shows meaningfully stronger predictive accuracy (88.8% vs 82.2%) with universally excellent state performance. Headroom Constraints: However, HSI consumes more daily range upfront (81.5% vs 68.8%), leaving tighter reward:risk ratios for Bar 2 entries (0.90× vs 1.20×). Structural Differences: HSI exhibits bear bias (1.08× frequency ratio) while ES shows bull bias (1.23×). HSI patterns demonstrate symmetric bull/bear behavior while ES shows asymmetry. Implementation Tradeoff: HSI offers superior directional confidence but requires more sophisticated entry timing or alternative stop placement to overcome sub-1× Bar 2 reward:risk constraints.

Recommended Next Steps

This study establishes exceptional directional correlation (88.8%), identifies uniformly excellent states (all 6 high-frequency states >80% accuracy), quantifies headroom constraints (0.46× pattern multiple, 0.90× Bar 2 multiple), and validates IBS impact. The sub-1× Bar 2 reward:risk suggests investigating: (1) Alternative entry timing — waiting for pullbacks to Bar 2's midpoint or low after pattern confirmation to improve R:R, (2) Wider stop placement — using pattern extremes or ATR-based stops rather than Bar 2 range to achieve positive expectancy, (3) State filtering — focusing on inside bar states (±5) which offer superior 1.16-1.27× Bar 2 multiples despite lower frequency, (4) Context dependency — whether accuracy or headroom varies by gap size, prior day type, or volatility regime, and (5) Time-to-target analysis — when during the session these patterns typically resolve to daily extremes for optimal profit-taking.
