Methodology: A MIG (Micro-Gap) Reversal occurs when price creates a micro-gap between consecutive bars (3+ in a row) but does so AGAINST the trend defined by the 20 EMA. Bull MIG Reversals = bull micro-gap (Low[0] > High[2]) with the signal bar's low below 20 EMA (bears disappointed — "why are bulls so strong down here?"). Bear MIG Reversals = bear micro-gap (High[0] < Low[2]) with the signal bar's high above 20 EMA (bulls disappointed — "why are bears so strong up here?"). These are "disappointment setups" suggesting potential reversals.

Step 4: EMA Position Analysis — Where Are Setups Relative to Trend?

By definition, Bull MIG Reversals require the signal bar's LOW to be below 20 EMA (bears disappointed — "why are bulls so strong down here?"), while Bear MIG Reversals require the signal bar's HIGH to be above 20 EMA (bulls disappointed — "why are bears so strong up here?"). However, the signal bar's CLOSE can be on either side of the EMA. We also track position relative to the 200 EMA for strategic trend context.

The signal bar's close location relative to the 20 EMA will be tracked in performance analysis to determine if it impacts win rates.

Strategic Trend Context (200 EMA): Bull MIG Reversals show 59.9% below the 200 EMA, suggesting they often occur in downtrends or bearish environments (counter-trend longs — bears disappointed that bulls are showing strength in a downtrend). Bear MIG Reversals show 67.4% above the 200 EMA, occurring primarily in uptrends (counter-trend shorts — bulls disappointed that bears are showing strength in an uptrend). This asymmetry is notable — bear setups trigger more often in bullish environments than bull setups do in bearish environments.

Timing Distribution: The majority of MIG Reversals (82.7%) occur AFTER the first hour of trading (10:30 EST onward), with only 17.3% appearing in the opening hour. This suggests the pattern is more common during mid-session and afternoon trading when trends have established and potential exhaustion/reversal dynamics develop. Opening hour MIG Reversals may warrant separate analysis due to their distinct volatility and gap-related behavior.

Phase 1 Summary: Setup Frequency Established

Population Characteristics: 1,755 MIG Reversal setups across 1,017 trading days (1.73 avg per day). Bulls slightly outnumber bears (902 vs 853, 51.4% / 48.6%) but distribution is balanced. Setups occur on 80.8% of trading days, with 1-2 setups per day being most common (68.0%). Pattern length is concentrated in 3-bar setups (70.8%), with 92.3% being 3-4 bar patterns.

Close Location Split: Bull setups: 39.6% close below 20 EMA, 60.4% close above. Bear setups: 41.3% close above 20 EMA, 58.7% close below. This ~40/60 split in close location will be tracked in performance analysis.

Next Steps: With frequency established, the next phase will analyze win rate, MFE/MAE, target hit rates, and time-to-target metrics. The EMA close location split will be a variable to track in win rate analysis.
